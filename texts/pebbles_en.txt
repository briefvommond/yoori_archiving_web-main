《The Sea Seeping into the White Pebbles》




Two suns
silence and clamor
thirst,
a person forever dancing

—from Baek Eun-sun's poem Silence and Clamor¹




Watching the waves rise and fall along the shoreline, I find myself thinking about the difference between approaching something and holding it.

The moment we step toward the sea, the foam scatters, dissolves, collapses, and finally disappears into the sand. Even if we scoop up seawater in our hands, we can never capture the wave itself—only still water remains. Perhaps approaching the sea, or trying to contain it, is less about reaching it than about gazing toward the shimmering horizon that forever recedes beyond our grasp: sensing the emotions carried by a shipwrecked wind, and enduring the tireless labor of waves that endlessly surge above and beneath the water's surface.

Rather than measuring the distance between these acts, perhaps what matters is exploring the possibilities that exist in between.

Accepting that this is a long and patient journey, we begin to attend to the textures that catch our eyes, the sounds that linger in our ears, and the fleeting sensations that brush against our fingertips. We watch the countless gestures of waves that never learn to stop. And when the waters grow calm and the wind finally settles, we turn our gaze to the white pebbles resting quietly along the shore, humbly receiving the stillness that arrives after the clamor.

The Sea Seeping into the White Pebbles draws from Baek Eun-sun's poem Silence and Clamor—its two suns, silence and clamor, and its thirst for an eternal dance—to reflect on what it means to live and act as an artist.

The heart and actions of making art may or may not coincide with what we call "practice," yet they resemble the same quiet momentum that has allowed white pebbles to endure beside the sea through immeasurable time, sharing something of the waves' relentless, unvanishing movements.

In the poem, an aimless wandering unfolds between choosing silence and embracing clamor through a succession of gestures that often resist explanation. The poet's finely tuned language repeatedly gathers into sentences only to collapse again. The fragments of imagery that emerge drift unstably before settling, each into its own unexpected form.

This exhibition presents scenes born from that ongoing journey between silence and clamor. It speaks of the silence that precedes every outburst, and of the clamor that quietly grows from within silence itself.

The worlds created here by four artists and one poet are not fragments cut from finite moments, but unfinished territories where meaning is compressed within an endless continuity. Yet it would be too simple to dismiss these scenes as merely process, or to say they remain incomplete.

Instead, they move toward the elusive realm of perception that is so easily mistaken for illusion. They gather silent histories that cannot be spoken in any other language. They face emotions that recede even as we try to hold them, tracing the presence of what quietly disappears, while revealing the weight and density that time and experience leave behind.

No single event or image can ever fully contain these moments. They inevitably carry a longing for what comes next, an enduring thirst that asks the empty space between scenes to be filled.

"As no words can fully reveal silence and clamor," the arrival of the next scene becomes inevitable. Like waves that never truly exhaust themselves, we continue moving—bridging the spaces between scenes and piecing together the fragments of drifting images.

Even if those gestures occupy only the briefest instant, and even if they are restless movements driven by a longing for eternity.



Written by Ryu Hee-yeon (Independent Curator)



¹ Baek Eun-sun, A Film Made of Scenes No One Remembers (Seoul: Hyundae Munhak, 2019), pp. 110–114.
