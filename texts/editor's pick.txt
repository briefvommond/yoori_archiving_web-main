Yoori: Shapeless Loop



Everything has a name, and all entities that are grasped within the cognitive map of human perception—including phenomena that are visible or invisible—are defined through the signs of language used for communication. Yoori delves into the dense gaps of this vast world and captures thoughts and sensations that lie beyond the reach of human systems of signs in the form of paintings, objects, books and paper, or material bodies that are neither books nor paper.

What Shapeless Loop binds together are fragments of everyday moments—nameless emotions and amorphous masses that merely drift by and vanish. Focusing on the theme of “connection” between such things, the artist expands the realm beyond language by giving shape to otherwise inexpressible streams of thought, either as translucent books or as forms painted onto canvases.

The exhibition also presents a substantial selection from the artist’s long-running “Artist Book” project alongside the paintings, offering viewers an opportunity to encounter the artist’s thoughts in a more tangible way. Through works realized in diverse forms and materials, one can discover traces of humanity that, in the artist’s own words, are “not fixed beings but beings that always exist in-between,” and find loops that weave together fragments of life.
